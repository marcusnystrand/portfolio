Kryohabitat
In 2017 a group of scientists successfully froze sperm from 16 different coral species, using liquid nitrogen. The process is called cryopreservation and the purpose is to “save” the species before they go extinct. The idea is to thaw them in the future and reintroduce them to their natural habitat, but that requires the global warming to be reduced. While waiting for this, the cryohabitat will be their new home.
I made digital sculptures of these 16 coral species an 3D-printed them for a physical interpretation of the cryohabitat. This was part of an exhibition at Svenskt Tenn in Stockholm January 2017. Feel free to download the model pack HERE.
